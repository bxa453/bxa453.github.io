Introduction

Mexico was colonized by Spain mid 16 century. Administered as the Viceroyalty of New Spain for three centuries, Mexico got their freedom in the nineteenth century. One of their first election races were held in 2000.Key note here is that Vicente Fox of the National Action Party (PAN) - beat the gathering in government, the Institutional Revolutionary Party (PRI).

In the late 2008 the global financial crisis caused an impact on Mexico. Social concerns incorporate low wages, high underemployment, and unjust pay. Since 2007, Mexico's powerful drug-trafficking organizations have left thousands of drug-related homicides.

Communications

Broadcast Media

Mexico has 821 TV stations and 1,745 radio stations. The Televisa group once had a virtual monopoly in TV broadcasting. Mexico was the first country in Latin America to transition from analog to digital. This would let the viewers have better image quality and better audio quality.
